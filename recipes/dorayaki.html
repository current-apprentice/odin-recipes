Dorayaki Recipe

Description

Dorayaki is a popular Japanese dessert consisting of two fluffy pancakes filled with a sweet red bean paste, known as "anko." These delightful treats are often enjoyed as a snack or dessert and are loved for their soft texture and deliciously sweet filling.

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Sweet red bean paste (anko)

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, beat the eggs and sugar together until light and fluffy.
  2. Add the honey and vanilla extract to the egg mixture, and continue beating until well combined.
  3. In a separate bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until smooth and well incorporated.
  5. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease with oil or butter
  6. Pour about 1/4 cup of the batter onto the skillet to form a pancake, and cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook until golden brown on both sides.
  7. Repeat with the remaining batter to make additional pancakes.
  8. Once all the pancakes are cooked, spread a generous amount of sweet red bean paste onto one pancake, then sandwich another pancake on top to form a dorayaki.
  9. Serve the dorayaki warm or at room temperature, and enjoy the delicious combination of fluffy pancakes and sweet red bean paste filling!
